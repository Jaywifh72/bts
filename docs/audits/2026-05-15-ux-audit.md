# CineCanon — UX audit (2026-05-15)

Live audit of the dev server at `localhost:3000` across the homepage, films
index + detail, crew detail, VFX detail, awards index, search/ask, and gear.
Findings are ranked **P0** (ship-blocking on first impression), **P1**
(visible friction for working pros), **P2** (polish).

Each finding has a concrete file/path so the fix is one-search-away.

## TL;DR — top 6 wins

1. **Add jump-nav to film/crew detail pages.** A film page emits 30+ H2/H3
   sections in one scroll. A DP looking for the lighting section can't find
   it without scrolling past 15 unrelated sections.
2. **Fix empty `alt=""` on hero images** across `/films/[slug]` and
   `/crew/[slug]`. Real a11y violation, easy fix.
3. **Lowercase department headings** ("camera", "electric", "sound") look
   like rendering bugs. Title-case them.
4. **Collapse Featured Productions / Recently Updated** on the homepage —
   Anora literally appears twice within 800px of scroll.
5. **Disambiguate or merge References / Queries / Ask** in the nav. Three
   discovery entry points compete for the same intent.
6. **Make the homepage example chips clickable** — they're plain text
   inside a "Try:" label right now; chip-style buttons would invite the
   first interaction.

## P0 — Accessibility / ship-blockers

### A11y-1 · Hero images have empty `alt=""`

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/ProductionDetail.tsx` (article
header `<Image>`), and `apps/web/app/crew/[slug]/page.tsx` (person photo).

**What I saw:** On `/films/dune-part-two-2024` the hero `<img alt="">`. On
`/crew/roger-deakins` the portrait `<img alt="">`. Both are SIGNIFICANT
visual elements that screen readers should announce.

**Fix:** alt should be the production title (`alt={production.title}`) or
the person's name. If the image is purely decorative (e.g. abstract
backdrop), wrap behind `role="presentation"` + `alt=""` is fine — but in
both cases above the image carries information.

**Effort:** 15 min. Two-file edit.

### A11y-2 · `/films/[slug]` lacks any landmark to jump-skip the long crew lists

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/ProductionDetail.tsx`.

**What I saw:** 30+ H2/H3 sections on Dune Part Two between the hero and
the Scenes section. The skip-link in the layout jumps to `#main-content`,
which is the *top* of all this. A screen-reader user has to traverse
every heading to reach the part they care about.

**Fix:** Add a section landmark / jump-nav. Two approaches:

- **Cheap:** Render an in-page TOC at the top of the article (`<nav
  aria-label="Sections">` with anchor links to each `id=`-bearing section).
  Could be a `<details open>` so it doesn't dominate visually.
- **Better:** Sticky right-rail TOC on `lg:` viewports. The page is already
  data-dense; using the extra horizontal space is appropriate here.

**Effort:** 2-3 hours.

## P1 — Information architecture & visible friction

### IA-1 · Three discovery entry points compete in the nav

**Where:** `apps/web/components/nav/TopNav.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Top nav has 11 links including **References / Queries /
Ask**. From a working pro's perspective:

- `/ask` — natural-language search with example chips ✓
- `/search` — keyword search (exists, separate route, not in nav)
- `/references` — cross-cited sources index
- `/queries` — reference queries ("ALEXA 65 + Sphero", etc.)

A new visitor can't tell from the labels which of these does what. The
homepage even has a `/ask` widget inline ("Try: Deakins photochemical
workflow") that's *different from* the `/search` widget elsewhere.

**Fix options (pick one):**

- **A.** Merge `/queries` into `/ask` as a "Saved queries" tab on `/ask`.
  Drop `/queries` from the nav. Keep `/references` as its own thing
  (it's structurally different — sources, not queries).
- **B.** Group under a single "Search" mega-dropdown with three modes
  (Ask • Saved queries • References).
- **C.** Rename to be intent-led: "Ask the corpus", "Saved queries",
  "Source library". Less ambiguous than the current labels.

**Effort:** A: 1 hr (just removing the nav link + adding a tab). B: 4-6 hr.
C: 30 min.

### IA-2 · Homepage repeats content within one scroll

**Where:** `apps/web/app/page.tsx` — Featured Productions + Recently
Updated sections.

**What I saw:** Anora appears in **both** the "Featured Productions" rail
and the "Recently updated" rail within 800px of scroll. So does Conclave.
This is editorial duplication, not a code bug, but it reads as a stutter.

**Fix:** Two options:

- **A.** Recently Updated explicitly *excludes* anything in Featured —
  query already has the data, just `WHERE slug NOT IN (featured.slugs)`.
- **B.** Drop "Recently Updated" entirely. Featured is curated-editorial;
  Recently Updated is metadata-noise on a curation-focused site.

**Effort:** A: 30 min. B: 15 min.

### IA-3 · 30+ headings on a film page with no skim affordance

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/ProductionDetail.tsx`.

**What I saw on `/films/dune-part-two-2024`:**

> At a glance · Studios · camera · electric · sound · art · wardrobe ·
> makeup hair · production · post · vfx · direction · writing · music ·
> stunts · Visual Effects · Awards · Release dates · Shooting locations
> (× 3 sub) · Lighting (× 2 setups) · Pipeline · Stunts · Lab & finishing
> · Videos · Scenes & Equipment

Page is 1742px (≈ 2 viewport heights). Conceptual density is way higher
than vertical density — a DP looking for "what was the color pipeline?"
has to either Ctrl-F or scroll-and-hunt.

**Fix:** Already covered by **A11y-2** above (jump-nav). Same change
solves both.

### IA-4 · Department headings are lowercase (looks like a bug)

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/ProductionDetail.tsx` — the
section that renders crew by department iterates over
`role_category` values which are lowercase ENUMs (`camera`, `electric`,
`sound`, `art`, `wardrobe`, `makeup hair`, `production`, `post`, `vfx`,
`direction`, `writing`, `music`, `stunts`).

**What I saw:** `<h2>camera</h2>`, `<h2>makeup hair</h2>`. Reads like a
debug artifact next to the title-cased headings elsewhere on the page.

**Fix:** A label map (same pattern as `lib/award-labels.ts`):

```ts
const DEPARTMENT_LABELS: Record<RoleCategory, string> = {
  camera: 'Camera',
  electric: 'Electric / Lighting',
  sound: 'Sound',
  art: 'Art Department',
  wardrobe: 'Costume',
  makeup_hair: 'Makeup & Hair',  // also fixes the space-vs-underscore inconsistency
  // ...
};
```

**Effort:** 30 min. Pattern already established by `award-labels.ts`.

### IA-5 · "8 offices" is rendered as an H2 on /vfx/[slug]

**Where:** `apps/web/app/vfx/[slug]/page.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Framestore page has `<h2>8 offices</h2>` as a section
heading. That's a *stat phrased as a heading* — confusing IA. The
heading should describe the *kind* of content; the count is a sub-label.

**Fix:** `<h2>Offices</h2>` with the count rendered as a `<p>` or
`<SectionHeader label="…" heading="Offices" />` (matching the pattern
elsewhere). Same applies to "8 setups across 2 scenes" on film pages.

**Effort:** 20 min.

### IA-6 · Duplicate H1 + H2 with the same text

**Where:** `apps/web/app/vfx/[slug]/page.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Framestore page has both `<h1>Framestore</h1>` (the title
in the hero) AND `<h2>Framestore</h2>` (a section heading further down).
The H2 is announcing what's clearly the SAME entity name back to a
screen reader as "Framestore, heading level 2" right after the H1.

**Fix:** Either drop the redundant H2 or change it to describe the
*section's content* ("Overview", "About", "Summary", whatever the body
below is). Probably the inline `<SectionHeader>` for the summary
paragraph block — switch its prop or remove.

**Effort:** 10 min.

## P1 — Content / discoverability

### CO-1 · Homepage example queries are plain text, not clickable chips

**Where:** `apps/web/app/page.tsx` hero.

**What I saw:** Under the Ask widget, a "Try:" label with three text
fragments — "Deakins photochemical workflow", "ALEXA 65 anamorphic",
"magic-hour 2023". They're styled but not interactive — the visitor has
to retype them into the search box.

**Fix:** Render each as a button/link that pre-fills the search input or
navigates directly to `/ask?q=...`. Same pattern used on /ask itself.

**Effort:** 30 min.

### CO-2 · Featured Productions cards include full TMDb synopsis

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/ProductionCard.tsx` (or the
homepage rail variant).

**What I saw:** Cards show ~200-character synopsis paragraphs. On the
6-card Featured rail this means ~1200 chars of body copy per row when
the user is trying to *visually pattern-match* against title + format.

**Fix:** Truncate to ~120 chars with ellipsis in the card; full synopsis
lives on `/films/[slug]`. Even better: replace with a *technical
shorthand* line ("Cinematography: Greig Fraser · IMAX 70mm + LF") since
that's the differentiator for this audience.

**Effort:** 1 hr.

### CO-3 · `/search` page is sparse and undescribed

**Where:** `apps/web/app/search/page.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Title "Search", placeholder "Search…", no explanatory
description, no examples. Bounce-prone if a visitor lands here from a
search-engine result.

**Fix:** Match the `/ask` page's pattern — short paragraph explaining
"keyword search across films/people/gear; for tonal/structural queries
try Ask." Add 2-3 example links.

**Effort:** 30 min.

### CO-4 · `/awards` page is 3430px tall — too much in one scroll

**Where:** `apps/web/app/awards/page.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Hero (stats) → filter form → 11 per-org panels (each with
6 recent wins). That's ~70 list items the visitor has to scan unless
they engage the filter. Most visitors will skip past everything.

**Fix:** Default the page to **filter-mode results only** when a filter
query string is present (already implemented), otherwise show a
compressed view: top-3 per-org summary chips with "see all" expandable
panels. Saves ~2000px of unnecessary scroll on the default view.

**Effort:** 1-2 hr.

## P2 — Polish

### PO-1 · Hero region on film pages doesn't show recipient on awards section

**Where:** `apps/web/components/productions/AwardsList.tsx` (already
extended in commit `95a048c6`).

**What I saw:** Works correctly now — VFX house + stunt company recipients
link properly. No issue. Listed here just to confirm the post-rebrand
change landed cleanly.

### PO-2 · Bookmark star (★) in nav has no label

**Where:** `apps/web/components/nav/TopNav.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Final nav slot is a `★` glyph linking to `/bookmarks`.
First-time visitors can't tell it's a bookmarks shortcut.

**Fix:** Either `aria-label="Bookmarks"` (cheapest) or a small text
label next to the glyph on `sm:` and up.

**Effort:** 5 min.

### PO-3 · Footer is dense at the bottom of every page

**Where:** `apps/web/components/nav/Footer.tsx`.

**What I saw:** 5 columns × 8-11 links each = ~50 footer links. On most
sites the footer is "About / Privacy / Twitter" + maybe a sitemap; here
it's a full IA second nav. That's *fine* for a reference site but
worth a deliberate decision.

**Fix:** No action required; flagging for awareness. If you want to
slim it, group cross-cut links under a `<details>` "More sections"
toggle.

### PO-4 · The filter form on `/awards` is good but doesn't preserve scroll on submit

**Where:** `apps/web/app/awards/page.tsx` form.

**What I saw:** Submitting the filter form (GET → URL change → server
re-render) scrolls back to the top of the page. The user clicked the
filter button which is below the hero; they'd expect to stay near the
results.

**Fix:** Add `#results` anchor to the filter `form action="#results"` and
to the results `<section id="results">`. One-line change.

**Effort:** 5 min.

### PO-5 · No visible loading state on `/films` filter changes

**Where:** `apps/web/app/films/page.tsx`.

**What I saw:** Changing the filter dropdowns navigates to a new URL;
during the SSR roundtrip there's no skeleton/spinner. On a slow DB this
could feel broken.

**Fix:** Already have `app/films/loading.tsx` for cold loads — that
serves on filter changes too since the URL changes. Verify it actually
fires; if not, add `loading.tsx` at the right segment level.

**Effort:** 30 min if it doesn't already fire.

## P2 — Mobile responsiveness

**Note:** the in-conversation browser tool can't actually resize the
inner viewport (window resize honored, but `window.innerWidth` stayed at
3062 regardless of requested 390×844). Recommend you test on a real
phone or Chrome DevTools' device mode for these checks:

- **Nav drawer** (hamburger): code-confirmed wired (TopNav uses
  `lg:hidden` + `useState` drawer). Live-test it triggers below 1024px.
- **Awards filter form**: 5-column grid (`lg:grid-cols-5`,
  `sm:grid-cols-2`). Verify SM stacks readably.
- **Film tech panel**: `lg:grid-cols-3 sm:grid-cols-2`. Likely fine.
- **Featured Productions rail**: usually card-grids that wrap. Verify
  cards don't stretch awkwardly at 390px.
